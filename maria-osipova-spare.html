Who you'll meet

Three delegates worth the trip to Denver for Spare

Jennifer Hoffman
City & County Manager
City & County of Broomfield

As a city and county manager in Colorado — the very state where the summit is being held — Jennifer Hoffman is precisely the type of public sector decision-maker responsible for transit and mobility service delivery that Spare's platform is built to serve. Municipal governments managing paratransit obligations under ADA requirements are under constant pressure to reduce per-ride costs while improving service quality, and Spare's SaaS model addresses that directly. Her operational remit likely includes evaluating or overseeing transportation contracts, making her a warm entry point into the public sector buyer network across North America. A single conversation with Hoffman could open doors to regional transit procurement conversations that would otherwise take months of cold outreach to reach.

Conversation opener "Broomfield is in an interesting spot — growing fast but still managing the transit demands of a community that needs flexible, on-demand service. Are you finding the gap between traditional fixed-route transit and what residents actually need is widening?"
Kelly Darnell
COO
American Clean Power

American Clean Power represents a sector increasingly investing in workforce mobility and site access logistics as it scales renewable energy infrastructure across North America — construction crews, technicians, and remote site workers all need coordinated, demand-responsive transportation that fixed-route systems can't serve. Spare's microtransit platform is exactly the kind of flexible, bookable mobility layer that could support clean energy workforce logistics at scale. As COO, Darnell owns the operational build-out of a rapidly growing industry, and transportation access for a distributed workforce is a real friction point. Engaging her at Denver positions Spare not just in the transit agency vertical but as a broader mobility infrastructure partner for growing industries.

Conversation opener "As clean power infrastructure scales out into more remote and distributed locations, getting workers to sites reliably seems like it could become a real operational constraint — how are you thinking about workforce mobility as you grow?"
Chris Spanjaard
SVP & COO
Purolator

Purolator is one of Canada's largest courier and logistics networks, and as a fellow British Columbia-rooted operation, Spare shares geographic and operational DNA with this company's challenges around last-mile efficiency and dynamic routing across both urban and rural corridors. Spanjaard as COO is accountable for the operational infrastructure that determines cost-per-delivery and service reliability — the same metrics Spare optimizes for transit agencies. While Purolator's core business is parcels, the convergence of people-mobility and goods-mobility technology is accelerating, and Spare's platform architecture could be highly relevant to their thinking on demand-responsive fleet operations. This is also a natural Canadian enterprise relationship that could extend Spare's brand credibility in its home market.

Conversation opener "There's a lot of crossover happening between how logistics networks and transit networks think about dynamic routing and demand-responsiveness — are you seeing that convergence showing up in how Purolator thinks about its own fleet operations?"
