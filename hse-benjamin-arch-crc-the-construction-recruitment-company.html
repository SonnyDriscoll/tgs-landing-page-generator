On stage in Birmingham

Keynotes that make CRC The Construction Recruitment Company relevant to every HSE leader in the room

RS
Rory Sutherland
Vice Chair, Ogilvy UK
Sutherland's work on behavioural science and the psychology of decision-making gives CRC a powerful frame: the choice of who to hire is itself a safety-critical decision shaped by cognitive bias and system design. His session creates the perfect opening to position CRC's rigorous vetting and trade-specific matching as a behavioural corrective — reducing the unconscious shortcuts that lead construction businesses to hire under-qualified or unsafe workers.
IL
Ian Leslie
Author, Curious
Leslie's focus on how people learn and change speaks directly to the challenge construction businesses face when integrating new tradespeople into safety-critical environments — and why cultural fit and safety awareness must be assessed before hire, not after induction. For CRC, this keynote contextualises their pre-screening and candidate matching process as a human factors intervention, not just a transactional service.
MO
Maeve O'Loughlin
HSE Transformation & Culture Consultant
O'Loughlin's expertise in safety culture transformation resonates strongly with CRC's value proposition: that placing the right tradespeople — those who already embody a safety-first mindset — is one of the most impactful levers an organisation can pull to shift culture from the ground up. Her session primes HSE leaders to think about workforce composition as a culture variable, making CRC's specialist placement model an obvious solution to discuss.
